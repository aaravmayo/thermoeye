Project Overview

THERMO EYE uses a drone-mounted camera and AI-based computer vision to analyze normal video and detect crowd density in real time. It visualizes dense zones, triggers alerts, and supports fast decision-making for crowd safety.

Quick facts

Portable (Raspberry Pi) AI-based density detection Real-time alerts Low-cost & scalable

How it works

1
Frame Ingestion
Read live video from drone / file / IP camera
2
Pre-filter & Pan Detection
Noise reduction (Gaussian blur) and detect extreme camera motion
3
Background Subtraction & Motion Extraction
MOG2 / adaptive background learning to separate moving crowd pixels
4
Binary Mask & Morphology
Threshold confident motion pixels and refine with close/open operations
5
Grid Partitioning & Density Calculation
Divide frame into cells and compute white-pixel fraction per cell
6
Temporal Smoothing & People Proxy
Smooth across 15 frames and estimate density-based people proxy
7
Visualization & Alerts
Apply color map, overlay on video, and emit MQTT alerts if thresholds exceeded
